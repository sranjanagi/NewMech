Forced vibration of single degree of freedom system- Base excitation


Introduction

In many cases, dynamical system is excited by motion of the support point or base as shown in the Fig 1. The base excitation system may be of vehicle suspension system or earthquake response of a structure or a performance of a precision machine located close proximity of a vibrating structure as shown in Fig 2. The motion of the base causes the main system to vibrate as a function of excitation frequency and amplitude. Base excitation models the behaviour of a vibration isolation system and is applicable in the analysis of preventing the excitations from passing from a vibrating base through its mount into a structure.

To study the response of a dynamic system for base excitation, given real system will be modelled as a equivalent single degree spring-mass system as shown in Fig 3 and it is excited by a harmonic input y(t)=Y sin?t applied at the base. The proposed single degree spring-mass system can be thought of as representing a single mode of vibration in a real system, whose natural frequency and damping coefficient coincide with that of proposed spring-mass system.

Differential equation of motion of the system is given by,
$m\overset{}{\ddot x+c\overset.x+kx=c\dot y+ky}\;$... (1)
Since support is excited by a harmonic function y(t)=Y sin?t, substituting y and in equation 1,
$m\overset{}{\ddot x+c\overset.x+kx=c\omega Y\cos\left(\omega t\right)}\;+kY\;\sin\left(\omega t\right)$ ... (2)
Equation 2 can be rewritten as,
$m\overset{}{\ddot x+c\overset.x+kx=Y\sqrt{k^2+(c\omega)^2}}\;\sin(\omega t+\alpha)$... (3)

where,
$\alpha=\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{c\omega}k\right)=\tan^{-1}\left(2\varsigma\frac\omega{\omega_n}\right)$
The general solution of this mathematical model consists of two parts, the complementary function which is the solution of the homogenous equation, and the particular integral. The particular solution to the preceding equation is a steady-state oscillation of same frequency ? as that of excitation. We can assume particular solution to be of the form $x=X\sin(\omega t+\alpha-\phi)$... (4)
Where X is the amplitude of oscillation and is the phase of the displacement with respect to the exciting force.
By substituting the equation 4 in equation 3, steady state amplitude X and phase given as,

$X=\frac{Y\sqrt{k^2+(c\omega^2)}}{\sqrt{(k-m\omega^2)^2+(c\omega)^2}}$ ... (5)

$\phi=\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{c\omega}{(k-m\omega^2)}\right)$... (6)
